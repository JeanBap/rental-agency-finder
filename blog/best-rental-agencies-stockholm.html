Best Rental Agencies in Stockholm 2026: Complete Expat Guide

Published 1 May 2026 | 10 min read

Finding a Rental Agency in Stockholm

TL;DR

Compare 106 verified rental agencies in Stockholm, Sweden. Read tenant reviews, understand the Swedish rental queue, and avoid common scams.

Stockholm's rental market is one of Europe's most challenging to navigate. The city operates a dual system: first-hand contracts (forstahandskontrakt) through the municipal housing queue, and second-hand sublets (andrahandskontrakt) through private channels. The municipal queue has an average wait time of 9-12 years for central Stockholm, making agencies and private sublets essential for newcomers.

Understanding Stockholm's Rental System

Second-hand sublets typically cost SEK 10,000 to SEK 18,000 per month (EUR 850-1,500) for a one-bedroom in central areas. Swedish law allows landlords to charge up to 15% above the first-hand rent for furnished sublets. Contracts are usually 6-12 months with a one-month notice period. Be wary of scams: never pay a deposit before viewing a property in person or via a verified video call.

The Hyresnamnden (Rent Tribunal) handles disputes and can review rents for fairness. Tenants have strong protections against eviction, and rent increases are negotiated collectively through tenant associations (Hyresganstforeningen).

Popular Areas for Renters

Sodermalm attracts young creatives and professionals with its bars, vintage shops, and waterfront views. Ostermalm is the upscale option with embassy proximity and international schools. Kungsholmen offers a residential feel near City Hall with good metro access. Hammarby Sjostad is a modern eco-district popular with families. For more affordable options, Solna, Sundbyberg, and Nacka offer good connections via the T-bana (metro).

Avoiding Scams

Stockholm's tight market unfortunately breeds rental scams, particularly on platforms like Blocket. Legitimate agencies will never ask for payment before you have signed a contract and received keys. Always verify the sublessor actually has permission from their landlord to sublet, as unauthorized sublets can be terminated by the property owner.
