Best Rental Agencies in Vienna: A Complete Guide for 2026

Finding a Rental Agency in Vienna

Vienna consistently ranks among the world's most liveable cities, and its rental market reflects that appeal. Around 60% of Vienna's population rents, supported by one of Europe's strongest social housing systems. The city's Gemeindebau (municipal housing) programme provides affordable options, but private-market rentals remain the primary route for newcomers and expats.

Understanding Vienna's Rental Market

Private-market rents in Vienna range from EUR 800 to EUR 1,500 per month for a one-bedroom apartment in central districts (1st through 9th Bezirke). The Mietrechtsgesetz (Tenancy Law) provides strong tenant protections, including limits on rent increases for older buildings (Altbau). Newer buildings (Neubau, post-1945) have fewer restrictions, so rents vary significantly based on building age.

Most rentals require a deposit of three months' rent (Kaution) plus one to two months' commission (Provision) to the agency. A 2023 reform shifted commission costs to landlords for standard rentals, though some exceptions apply. Verify whether your agency charges tenant commission before signing.

Popular Districts for Renters

The 7th district (Neubau) and 8th (Josefstadt) attract young professionals with their cafes, boutiques, and cultural venues. The 2nd (Leopoldstadt) and 20th (Brigittenau) near the Donaukanal offer more affordable options with improving infrastructure. The 18th (Wahring) and 19th (Dobling) appeal to families seeking green spaces and international schools. The 3rd district (Landstrasse) near the Belvedere provides a balance of central location and residential calm.

What to Look for in a Vienna Agency

Austrian real estate agents must hold a Gewerbeberechtigung (trade licence). Look for membership in the Wirtschaftskammer (Chamber of Commerce) real estate section. Good Vienna agencies will explain the distinction between Altbau and Neubau rent regulations, handle Anmeldung (registration) guidance, and provide bilingual contracts for international tenants.
