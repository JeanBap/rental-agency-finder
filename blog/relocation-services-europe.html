Relocation Services in Europe: Finding Housing When Moving Abroad

Published 1 May 2026 by Yanni Papoutsis

Moving to a new country is complex enough without adding an apartment search in a language you may not speak, in a rental market you do not understand. Relocation services exist to bridge that gap. This guide explains what relocation agencies do, how much they cost, which European cities have the strongest relocation infrastructure, and when it makes sense to hire one versus searching independently.

What Relocation Agencies Actually Do

A relocation agency handles the housing search on your behalf. The typical service includes an initial consultation to understand your budget, preferred neighbourhoods, commute requirements, and lifestyle needs. The agent then pre-screens properties, arranges viewings (often accompanying you), negotiates lease terms, reviews contracts, and assists with utility setup and address registration.

Some agencies offer comprehensive packages that go beyond housing: school searches for families, spouse career support, cultural orientation, visa and permit assistance, and settling-in services like bank account setup and local SIM cards.

How Much Do Relocation Services Cost?

Pricing varies significantly by city and service level. Housing-only search packages typically range from EUR 1,000-3,000. Full relocation packages (housing + admin + settling-in) run EUR 2,500-6,000. Corporate relocation with family services can reach EUR 5,000-15,000.

In some markets, the landlord or the relocating company pays the fee. In Germany, the "Bestellerprinzip" law means the party that hired the agent pays, which is often the tenant. In the Netherlands and Scandinavian countries, corporate employers frequently cover relocation costs as part of the offer package.

Best European Cities for Relocation Services

Amsterdam

The Netherlands has one of Europe's most developed relocation industries, driven by multinational headquarters and the 30% tax ruling for skilled migrants. Dozens of agencies specialise in expat housing. The market is competitive and fast-moving; properties in Amsterdam rent within days. A relocation agent gives you access to listings before they hit public portals and handles the Dutch-language contracts.

Berlin and Munich

Germany's rental market is notoriously difficult for newcomers. Berlin requires Anmeldung (address registration), SCHUFA credit checks, and extensive documentation (income proof, employer letter, previous landlord reference). Relocation agents navigate these requirements and have relationships with landlords willing to rent to newcomers without German credit history.

Zurich and Geneva

Switzerland has one of Europe's tightest rental markets. In Zurich, it is common for 50+ applicants to compete for a single apartment. Relocation agencies in Switzerland often have exclusive access to properties through corporate partnerships. Given Swiss rental deposits (typically three months' rent held in a blocked bank account), professional guidance is valuable.

Dublin

Dublin's rental crisis means supply is extremely limited. Relocation agents can access pre-market listings and new developments before they go public. For tech workers relocating to Dublin's Silicon Docks, a relocation agency can cut search time from weeks to days.

Agencies in Dublin

Stockholm and Copenhagen

Scandinavian rental markets operate on queue systems (particularly Sweden's first-hand contract system, where wait times can exceed 10 years in Stockholm). Relocation agencies access the second-hand and private rental markets, which bypass the queue. In Copenhagen, agencies help navigate the Danish rental law distinctions between regulated and unregulated tenancies.

When to Use a Relocation Agency

A relocation agency is worth the investment when you are moving to a city with a tight rental market and limited English-language listings; when your employer offers a relocation budget; when you are moving with a family and need to coordinate school, housing, and admin simultaneously; or when you are relocating from outside Europe and cannot visit for viewings before your start date.

You can likely manage without one if you are moving to a city with plentiful supply and English-friendly agencies (Lisbon, Barcelona, Athens); if you have local contacts who can help with viewings and translation; or if you are on a tight personal budget and have time to search independently.

How to Choose a Relocation Agency

Check whether the agency is a member of the European Relocation Association (EuRA), which certifies quality standards. Read reviews from other relocating professionals. Ask for a clear breakdown of fees and what is included. Confirm whether the fee is fixed or depends on the property you end up renting. Ask how many properties they will show you and what happens if you do not find something suitable within the agreed timeframe.
