Best Rental Agencies in Budapest 2026: Complete Expat Guide

Finding a Rental Agency in Budapest

TL;DR

Compare 125 verified rental agencies in Budapest, Hungary. Read tenant reviews, understand Hungarian rental norms, and find trusted agencies across Buda and Pest.

Budapest offers some of the most affordable renting in a major European capital, combined with a vibrant cultural scene, thermal baths, and a rapidly growing digital economy. The city attracts a large community of digital nomads, students, and professionals relocating from Western Europe.

Understanding Budapest's Rental Market

Average rents in central Budapest range from EUR 500 to EUR 900 per month for a one-bedroom apartment. The Buda side (Districts I, II, XI, XII) tends to be quieter and more residential, while the Pest side (Districts V, VI, VII, XIII) offers more nightlife, restaurants, and urban energy. Hungarian rental contracts are typically for one year with a two-month deposit.

Unlike many Western European markets, Budapest has minimal rent regulation in the private sector. Prices are negotiable, especially for longer lease terms. Most agencies accept payment in Hungarian Forints (HUF) or Euros, though contracts are typically denominated in HUF.

Popular Districts for Renters

District V (Belvaros-Lipotvaros) is the historic centre with the highest rents but walkable access to everything. District VI (Terezvaros) and VII (Erzsebetvaros, the Jewish Quarter) offer a mix of renovated apartments and lively streets. District XIII (near Margaret Island) provides modern apartments at moderate prices. On the Buda side, District II (Rozsadomb) offers hillside living with panoramic views, while District XI (Ujbuda) near the university attracts students and young families.

What to Look for in a Budapest Agency

Look for agencies that provide bilingual contracts (Hungarian and English), as Hungarian-only contracts can create misunderstandings. Good agencies will handle utility transfers (kozmu atiras), explain the local tax obligations for tenants, and provide detailed inventories. Check whether the agency charges the tenant, the landlord, or both for commission (typically one month's rent).
